Executive Recommendation

Build a market intelligence copilot for physical goods. The first interface should combine Telegram for fast intake and alerts with a web dashboard for analysis, history, benchmarks, and team workflow.

Position the product as a decision layer, not an AI news summarizer.
1

Capture messy market inputs

Let users forward news, PDFs, screenshots, voice notes, analyst comments, price quotes, and field intelligence from the channels they already use.

2

Turn inputs into insight cards

Classify goods, regions, companies, time horizons, source quality, market direction, confidence, and likely business impact.

3

Validate with benchmarks

Compare each insight against prices, futures curves, inventory, trade flows, freight, FX, weather, policy, and substitute goods.

Key Product Features

The strongest product is built around speed, credibility, and decision usefulness. Every feature should help users answer what changed, why it matters, and what to watch next.

Forward-anything ingestion
Telegram forwarding, web upload, email forwarding, OCR for images and charts, PDF parsing, RSS feeds, and manual analyst notes.
Critical
Market entity extraction
Commodity, region, supplier, buyer, company, port, route, price unit, currency, date, forecast period, and substitute goods.
Critical
Insight cards
Structured output with headline, market impact, confidence score, horizon, reasoning, citations, and suggested benchmarks.
Critical
Source credibility scoring
Separate official data, exchange data, trade sources, mainstream media, social posts, and rumors. Track confirmation and historical reliability.
Critical
Watchlists and alerts
Users follow specific goods, regions, routes, companies, and risk factors. Alerts should trigger only when something is decision-relevant.
Critical
Executive briefings
Daily and weekly summaries covering top market moves, key news, biggest risks, changed assumptions, and watchpoints for the next period.
Critical
Scenario simulator
Model margin and cost sensitivity to commodity prices, freight, FX, tariffs, energy costs, and supplier constraints.
Phase 2
Team workflow
Save, tag, assign, comment, approve, and export insights so analyst teams and procurement teams can collaborate without losing context.

Benchmark Framework

The product becomes valuable when every market claim is checked against the right benchmark. The table below is the core benchmark library to design around.

Category Benchmarks Why It Matters
Price Spot price, futures price, local quote, import parity Validates whether news is already priced in or still developing.
Momentum 1D, 1W, 1M, 3M, and YoY change Separates noise from trend and helps rank urgency.
Volatility 30-day and 90-day realized volatility Shows risk level and whether hedging or faster action is needed.
Curve Contango, backwardation, front-month spreads Reveals supply tightness, storage economics, and near-term stress.
Supply Production, crop yield, mining output, refinery runs Connects operational reality to price and availability.
Inventory Warehouse stock, days of consumption, stock-to-use ratio Measures market cushion and potential price sensitivity.
Demand PMI, construction activity, industrial demand, retail demand Identifies whether price moves are demand-led or supply-led.
Trade Import/export volume, customs data, shipping routes Shows flow disruptions and regional arbitrage opportunities.
Cost Stack Energy, labor, freight, FX, financing, tariffs Explains landed cost, margin pressure, and procurement timing.
Risk Weather, sanctions, war, strikes, regulation, port disruption Captures asymmetric events that can move physical markets quickly.
Substitutes Relative pricing versus alternative goods Detects substitution pressure and demand transfer.
Sentiment News volume, analyst tone, trade chatter Adds context, but should never override hard benchmarks alone.

MVP Scope

Start narrow. Pick one market family and prove that the product creates decision advantage before expanding coverage.

Month 1

Telegram intake and insight cards

Users forward messages, links, images, and PDFs. The system creates structured insight cards with goods, region, impact, confidence, horizon, source, and reasoning.

Month 2

Web archive and search

Create a dashboard for searchable history, watchlists, tags, saved insights, corrections, and basic team review.

Month 3

Benchmarks and daily brief

Add price, momentum, inventory, trade, freight, and FX benchmarks for 10-30 priority goods. Generate daily and weekly executive briefs.

Phase 2

Scenario simulation and workflow

Model margin impact, landed cost, procurement scenarios, assignment, comments, approvals, exports, and analyst-quality source reliability tracking.

Recommended first vertical Choose one: agriculture and food commodities, metals and construction materials, energy and petrochemicals, or import/export goods for one country. Do not launch as a broad everything-market product.

Success Metrics

The product should be measured by decision usefulness, not by content volume. These targets help distinguish a serious B2B intelligence product from a generic AI summarizer.

Metric Good Target Interpretation
Forward-to-insight time Under 60 seconds Speed matters because market teams work in real time.
Insights with source citation 100% Citation is mandatory for trust, audits, and correction.
Entity extraction accuracy 90%+ after tuning Bad extraction destroys search, alerts, and benchmark mapping.
User saves or forwards insight 20-30%+ Shows whether insights are useful enough to circulate.
Weekly active paid users 50%+ of paid seats Serious market intelligence should become a weekly habit.
Alert precision 70%+ marked useful Low-quality alerts create churn quickly.
Daily brief open rate 50%+ for serious B2B users A strong brief becomes part of the operating rhythm.
Time saved per user 3-5 hours per week Supports ROI-based pricing and enterprise sales.

Data and Source Strategy

Start with official and public data sources, then add paid or licensed feeds as the product becomes commercially validated.

World Bank Commodity Markets Commodity price data and market outlook context. USDA WASDE Agriculture supply, demand, and stock forecasts. EIA Open Data Energy market data, petroleum, gas, power, and related indicators. CME Market Data Exchange market data and futures benchmarks, subject to licensing. Telegram Bot API Practical intake and alert channel for early product distribution.

Investor View

The defensible asset is not summarization. It is a market-specific knowledge system that learns which signals, sources, and benchmarks actually matter for each category of goods.

Moat

Proprietary goods taxonomy, source reliability history, benchmark mapping, user feedback, and a growing archive of decision-grade market interpretations.

Distribution

Telegram, email, and web dashboards match how market professionals already exchange time-sensitive information.

Commercial wedge

Start with paid teams in procurement, trading, import/export, manufacturing, and investment research where time savings and risk reduction are measurable.
